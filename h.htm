About the book

The Wandering Pages follows Emmett, a travelling bookseller who wanders coastal towns and mountain roads, trading books for stories. Each town's people bring a new chapter to the novel — and to Emmett's life — as he pieces together an unexpected past and learns the meaning of home.

Themes

  • Memory and identity
  • The transformative power of stories
  • Small communities and connection

Excerpt

He kept the map folded in the back of the book, lines creased like the creek beds he crossed. In the morning light the pages smelled of tea and rain. He didn't know which town would keep him, only that each book he traded left a memory behind like a pebble dropped into water.

Some books were given back as directions; some returned as warnings. Each one held a voice that whispered of where the next road might lead.
